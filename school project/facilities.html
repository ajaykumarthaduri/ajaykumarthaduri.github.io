Science Fair

Science fairs became viewed by many as a way to encourage and help students find their way into science and engineering career paths.Popularity of science fairs increased along with interest in the sciences after the world witnessed the use of the first two atomic weapons and the dawn of television. As the decade progressed, science stories in the news, such as Jonas Salk's vaccine for polio and the launch of Sputnik, brought science fiction to reality and attracted increasing numbers of students at every level to fairs.

Science fairs attract students at every level-elementary, middle and high school-to compete in science and technology activities. Science fairs also can allow for students with intense interest in the sciences to be paired with our faculty, so that the students have access to instruction and equipment that the local schools do not provide. This mentoring has been shown to be very important for student success.Science exhibitions play a crucial role in education. They bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application, making learning more tangible and exciting. They also encourage students to question, explore, and discover, which are fundamental aspects of scientific inquiry.
